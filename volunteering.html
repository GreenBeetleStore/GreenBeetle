Benefits of volunteering

· You learn from people who are different. Teenagers often find themselves in a bubble in which both family and acquaintances and friends are of the same style. When volunteering young people meet people who are very different from them not only in the socio-economic aspect but also in terms of culture and style. Thus, they broaden their view of the world a little.
· Development and increaseof generosity and solidarity. When young people do volunteering, they are allocating part of their free time to dedicate it to others. This will make them develop values such as generosity and solidarity.
· Encouraging empathy. By seeing and being with people who are in a complicated situation, whose resources are scarce or who live in a precarious way, this makes the volunteer grow in empathy, putting himself in their place and trying to help them.
· Develop commitment and perseverance. Volunteering requires a commitment since the volunteer must comply with the stipulated schedule and tasks and the work must be constant. In this aspect, the volunteer learns to take on responsibilities.
· Appreciate and thanks for what you have. Another very important benefit derived from volunteer work is that by seeing the misfortunes of others, you learn to value what you have and to minimize conflicts over insignificant things. In short, you learn to see life with different eyes and to take into account only what is important.
· Personal gratification. Another benefit of volunteering is the gratification of seeing your work well done and/or finished. Seeing the results of your help is very comforting.
· Gain experience. Whatever you do in volunteering activities, you can reflect it in your CV. Companies value and give importance to giving time to others selflessly.
