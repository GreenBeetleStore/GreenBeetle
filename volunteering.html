Volunteering

I actively collaborated in Nautical Clubs to bring light sailing to all audiences.
Among many more activities, I have summarized the most important ones below.

Benefits of volunteering

· You learn from people who are different. Teenagers often find themselves in a bubble in which both family and acquaintances and friends are of the same style. When volunteering young people meet people who are very different from them not only in the socio-economic aspect but also in terms of culture and style. Thus, they broaden their view of the world a little.
· Development and increaseof generosity and solidarity. When young people do volunteering, they are allocating part of their free time to dedicate it to others. This will make them develop values such as generosity and solidarity.
· Encouraging empathy. By seeing and being with people who are in a complicated situation, whose resources are scarce or who live in a precarious way, this makes the volunteer grow in empathy, putting himself in their place and trying to help them.
· Develop commitment and perseverance. Volunteering requires a commitment since the volunteer must comply with the stipulated schedule and tasks and the work must be constant. In this aspect, the volunteer learns to take on responsibilities.
· Appreciate and thanks for what you have. Another very important benefit derived from volunteer work is that by seeing the misfortunes of others, you learn to value what you have and to minimize conflicts over insignificant things. In short, you learn to see life with different eyes and to take into account only what is important.
· Personal gratification. Another benefit of volunteering is the gratification of seeing your work well done and/or finished. Seeing the results of your help is very comforting.
· Talk in public. The opportunity to express yourself in public facilitates the interrelation between other soft skills, and by virtue of practicing this quality is perfected.
· Gain experience. Whatever you do in volunteering activities, you can reflect it in your CV. Companies value and give importance to giving time to others selflessly.

Baptism of the sea, for children and teenagers

Sea baptisms are a very simple way of sailing by organizing groups that sail in a flotilla accompanied by a sailing instructor, without having to learn how to sail.
The main objective is to promote the practice of sailing by participating in a safe leisure activity, where participants are introduced to this discipline in a fun way and have the opportunity to perform the different tasks of sailing in a group.
It is applicable to all ages, but the majority of the participants were students from schools everywhere.

Light sailing courses

The sail, a tool to encourage teamwork. It is a cooperative sport where they work together inside the sailboat promoting solidarity. Beyond enjoying the sea and the activity itself, sailing trips bring several benefits to students.
The curriculum offered includes basic seafaring notions such as knot lessons, how to manage the rudder and sails and knowing how to position yourself in relation to the wind and to the space to get the most out of it.
First, the activity is developed in a fun way in small groups with a monitor, but the aim is for them to go to the boat alone. It is when the students change roles, see themselves alone in the middle of the sea, become empowered and develop their autonomy in confidence.

Adult weekend pass

Adults can find here a way to escape from everyday work and practice a fun and healthy activity, since they don't have time to follow regular courses.
That's why we created some timeless multi-session passes to practice sailing on weekends at everyone's convenience.
The individual begins to navigate in the modality accompanied by a monitor and when his learning progresses, he has the possibility of reaching the modality of navigating autonomously, accompanied by friends or family.

Introduction to windsurfing

In those years, windsurfing was beginning to be accessible to everyone and aroused great curiosity among young people and adults. It was a discipline that gradually became integrated in all sailing schools.
Windsurfing is a sport that improves balance and aerobic capacity. It also strengthens the joints and muscles of the hands, arms and back. It helps to develop muscle resistance. It strengthens self-esteem and personal autonomy. De-stress by the production of adrenaline. It is recommended for all ages as it can be practiced at different skill levels.
